Key Insights 5

Positive Insights



In 2018, the hate crime rate decreased by 2.1% compared to 2017. In 2017, the hate crime rate rose by 16.7% compared to the previous year. 


In 2019, Violent hate crimes committed by African Americans decreased by 5.7% vs 2018.  In 2018, similar hate crimes increased 6% over the previous year.



Negative Insights


Of all known offenders, Whites committed the highest number of violent hate crimes in 2020. In that year, violent hate crimes committed by White offenders increased by 34.0% compared to the previous two years. In 2019, violent hate crimes by White offenders increased by 8.2% and decreased by 0.6% in 2018.


Additionally, in California, hate crimes motivated by bias against gender/gender identity increased 51.5% in 2020 vs the previous year. In 2019, the rise in crimes by bias against gender/gender identity was 14.3% vs the previous year.
